## Act I: DISCOVERY (Chapters 1-8)

### Chapter 1: The Weight of Absence (4 scenes, ~1,400 words)

**Scene 1.1 — Night Office**
- **Location:** Maya's office, Meridian Tower, 2 AM
- **Characters:** Maya
- **Purpose:** Establish Maya's hollow world and avoidance
- **Summary:** Maya works alone at 2 AM, surrounded by blueprints. She's productive but empty. The city sprawls outside her window—her creation—but she feels nothing for it.

**Scene 1.2 — Memory of David**
- **Location:** Maya's office (memory)
- **Characters:** Maya, David (memory)
- **Purpose:** Introduce David through memory; establish their dynamic
- **Summary:** Maya remembers David asking what buildings remember. She never found an answer he was satisfied with. The question resurfaces now.

**Scene 1.3 — Phone Call**
- **Location:** Maya's office
- **Characters:** Maya, James (phone)
- **Purpose:** Introduce James; show Maya's isolation
- **Summary:** James calls. Maya ignores it. The phone buzzes again, then falls silent. She watches the city through her window, disconnected.

**Scene 1.4 — The Empty House**
- **Location:** Maya's home
- **Characters:** Maya
- **Purpose:** Show the cost of avoidance; the museum to the dead
- **Summary:** Maya finally goes home. The house is frozen in time—David and Lily's things untouched, dust collecting. She can't stay long. She leaves.

### Chapter 2: The Tomb Commission (3 scenes, ~1,400 words)

**Scene 2.1 — Tom's Entrance**
- **Location:** Chen Architecture office, conference room
- **Characters:** Maya, Tom Ridgewell, James
- **Purpose:** Introduce Tom; establish his character and desire
- **Summary:** Tom arrives with assistants and lawyers. He makes a demand: "I want you to build me a tomb. A beautiful one." Maya is both repulsed and fascinated.

**Scene 2.2 — The Commission**
- **Location:** Chen Architecture office, conference room
- **Characters:** Maya, Tom
- **Purpose:** Deepen Tom's character; trigger Maya's awareness of mortality
- **Summary:** Tom explains his desire for permanence, for something that outlasts him. Maya sees her own desires reflected back. She accepts the commission.

**Scene 2.3 — James's Observation**
- **Location:** Chen Architecture office, hallway
- **Characters:** James, Maya
- **Purpose:** Show James's concern; foreshadow the consequences
- **Summary:** James watches Maya accept the commission. He knows what it means—Maya is engaging with death, which means she's finally confronting her own.

## Act II: CONFRONTATION (Chapters 9-19)

### Chapter 9: The Pattern Emerges (4 scenes, ~1,600 words)

**Scene 9.1 — The Analytical Investigation**
- **Location:** Maya's studio
- **Characters:** Maya
- **Purpose:** Maya traces the pattern of memory loss
- **Summary:** Maya creates a map. Every manifestation. Every memory lost. The correlation is undeniable. Each building costs a memory. She's been trading herself for them.

**Scene 9.2 — The Wedding Venue**
- **Location:** Maya's studio / Parallel Ashford City
- **Characters:** Maya
- **Purpose:** Confirm the pattern with a crucial memory
- **Summary:** Maya sketches her wedding venue. The building appears. She tries to remember the wedding day. She can't. It's gone. Traded for a building she didn't need.

**Scene 9.3 — The Inventory**
- **Location:** Maya's studio
- **Characters:** Maya
- **Purpose:** Show Maya's growing panic as she catalogs her losses
- **Summary:** Maya inventories what's left. She remembers David. She remembers Lily. She remembers loving them. But details are fading. Colors, sounds, smells.

**Scene 9.4 — The Realization**
- **Location:** Maya's studio
- **Characters:** Maya
- **Purpose:** Maya understands the true cost
- **Summary:** She's been paying with herself. Every building, every memory of them, costs a memory of herself. She's disappearing.

- **Characters:** Maya
- **Purpose:** Maya tries to find a way around the rules
