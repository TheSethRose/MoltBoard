# The Weight of Shadows — Research Plan & Source List

## Project Overview

**Working Title:** The Weight of Shadows  
**Genre:** Literary Fiction / Magical Realism with Psychological Thriller Undertones  
**Author:** Seth Rose

---

## Research Overview

This research plan identifies key areas requiring research for authentic, grounded storytelling. The novel blends magical realism with psychological realism, requiring research in grief psychology, architecture, magical realism literature, and Pacific Northwest setting.

**Research Goals:**
1. Ensure authentic portrayal of grief and its psychological effects
2. Ground magical realism elements in architectural reality
3. Create believable Pacific Northwest setting
4. Achieve literary quality through genre awareness

---

## Research Areas

### 1. Grief Psychology & Bereavement

**Why Research This:**
Maya's journey is fundamentally about grief. The story must accurately portray the psychological effects of losing a spouse and child, the stages of grief, and healthy vs. unhealthy coping mechanisms.

**Key Questions:**
- What are the specific psychological effects of losing a spouse?
- How does grief differ when losing a child vs. other losses?
- What are healthy vs. unhealthy coping mechanisms?
- How does complicated grief manifest?
- What is the role of memory in grief processing?

**Research Topics:**
- Elisabeth Kübler-Ross's five stages of grief (and critiques)
- Complicated grief / prolonged grief disorder
- Continuing bonds theory (vs. letting go)
- Grief and identity transformation
- Disenfranchised grief
- Traumatic grief vs. anticipatory grief

**Specific Research Needs:**
- Psychology of spousal loss
- Psychology of parental grief (losing a child)
- Grief avoidance and its consequences
- The relationship between creativity and grief
- Memory changes during bereavement

---

### 2. Magical Realism as a Genre

**Why Research This:**
The novel operates within the magical realism tradition. Understanding this genre's conventions, techniques, and masters will inform narrative choices and tone.

**Key Questions:**
- What distinguishes magical realism from fantasy?
- How do master practitioners (García Márquez, Morrison, Allende) handle the balance?
- What makes magical realism feel literary rather than fantastical?
- How is the magical integrated without explanation?

**Research Topics:**
- Origins of magical realism in Latin American literature
- Key works: One Hundred Years of Solitude, Beloved, House of the Spirits
- The "lo real maravilloso" concept
- Magical realism in contemporary fiction
- The boundary between magical realism and fabulism

**Specific Research Needs:**
- Tone and voice in magical realism
- The matter-of-fact presentation of the impossible
- Social/political context in magical realism
- Gender and magical realism
- Architectural/urban magical realism

---

### 3. Architecture and Memory

**Why Research This:**
Maya is an architect, and architecture is central to the story's symbolism and magical system. Research will ground Maya's expertise and the manifestation phenomenon in architectural reality.

**Key Questions:**
- How does architecture affect emotion and memory?
- What is the philosophy of space?
- How do architects think about memory in design?
- What are the technical aspects of Maya's practice?

**Research Topics:**
- Phenomenology of architecture (Peter Zumthor, Juhani Pallasmaa)
- Memory and place attachment
- Sacred architecture and memorial design
- The psychology of built environments
- Women in architecture history

**Specific Research Needs:**
- Maya's specific architectural practice (commercial high-rises)
- Technical language of architecture (structural, materials, design)
- Contemporary architectural theory
- Memorial and monument design philosophy
- The relationship between architect and building

---

### 4. Pacific Northwest Setting

**Why Research This:**
The novel is set in Ashford City, a fictional version of the Pacific Northwest. Authentic setting details will ground the magical elements in a recognizable world.

**Key Questions:**
- What are the distinctive features of Pacific Northwest geography?
- What is the culture and vibe of cities like Seattle, Portland, Vancouver?
- How does the climate affect daily life and psychology?
- What architectural styles are distinctive to the region?

**Research Topics:**
- Pacific Northwest geography and climate
- Seattle and Portland urban character
- Pacific Northwest culture and identity
- Regional architecture (modern, eco-friendly, industrial)
- The relationship between Northwest natives and transplants

**Specific Research Needs:**
- Ashford City as fictional stand-in (Pacific Northwest feel)
- Climate details (rain, gray, light)
- Urban landscape (tech industry presence, geographic features)
- Local culture and character
- Natural environment (water, mountains, forests)

---

### 5. The Writing Craft

**Why Research This:**
The novel aims for literary quality. Studying craft in similar works will inform prose style, pacing, and structure.

**Key Questions:**
- How do literary fiction writers handle interiority?
- What are the techniques for handling time and memory in fiction?
- How do writers balance introspection with action?
- What makes prose feel "literary" vs. "genre"?

**Research Topics:**
- Interiority techniques in third person limited
- Time manipulation in fiction (past/present blending)
- Prose style in literary fiction
- Managing multiple timelines
- The relationship between voice and character

**Specific Research Needs:**
- Reading list of similar works (grief, magical realism, literary)
- Craft books on interiority and psychological fiction
- Techniques for handling unreliable memory
- Balancing poetic prose with forward momentum
